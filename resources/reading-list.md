# Reading List

Authoritative external sources behind the course, grouped by theme. The author's own instrument series (the DBA / DBA-MA documents, the Command Broker and TAF frameworks, the Democratic Safeguards papers) is referenced from within the modules and is not duplicated here; this list is the external foundation the method builds on. Current-example sources are dated because they age.

## Nuclear design-basis foundations

- U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission — the **Design Basis Threat** rule (10 CFR 73.1) and the design-basis concept in reactor licensing. The regulated origin of the discipline the course generalizes.
- NRC regulatory guides on digital instrumentation and control and verification (e.g., the RG 1.152 / 1.168–1.173 family), as the nuclear precedent for software V&V in safety systems.

## Consequence-driven engineering (CCE / CIE)

- Andrew Bochman and Sarah Freeman, *Countering Cyber Sabotage: Introducing Consequence-Driven, Cyber-Informed Engineering (CCE)* (Routledge, 2021) — the book-length statement of CCE.
- Idaho National Laboratory — CCE program materials and the Cyber-Informed Engineering (CIE) national initiative.
- Background on Michael Assante's role in originating consequence-driven thinking (industry retrospectives and the SANS/ICS community record).

## Standards: security, safety, and V&V

- **ISA/IEC 62443** series — industrial automation and control systems security: the zone-and-conduit model, Security Levels, and the seven Foundational Requirements. The standard the OT branch maps against.
- **IEEE 1012** — System, Software, and Hardware Verification and Validation. The V&V baseline the cognitive-AI framework extends.
- **IEC 61508** — Functional safety and Safety Integrity Levels (SIL); the analog for the three-tier criticality model.
- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0, 2023)** — the govern/map/measure/manage structure the governance analysis operationalizes.
- **NIST SP 800-82** — Guide to Operational Technology (OT) Security, for control-system context.
- The **Hybrid Formal Methods for GenAI in ICS** white paper and its "Grand Challenge" (the author's foundational paper) — the two-layer verification argument and the four candidate cognitive-layer verification techniques the IEEE V&V unit builds on. High-assurance precedents it draws on: **DARPA HACMS**, **seL4**, **CertiKOS**.

## Policy and critical-infrastructure framework

- The **National Infrastructure Protection Plan (NIPP)** and its sector structure; **PPD-21 (2013)** and its successor, the **2024 National Security Memorandum on Critical Infrastructure Security and Resilience (NSM-22)** — the framework whose omission is the founding gap.
- **NERC CIP** standards, including **CIP-014** (physical security) and **CIP-002** (BES cyber system categorization), as the one sector with a mandatory floor.
- **California SB 896 (Generative Artificial Intelligence Accountability Act, 2024)** — mandates a recurring joint risk analysis of generative-AI threats to California's critical energy infrastructure; a live, statutory instance of the course's consequence-first thesis.
- **Executive Order 14110 (2023, rescinded early 2025)** — the U.S. executive order on AI that addressed critical-infrastructure safety; cite as historical/superseded, and as an example of why an executive order is a weaker instrument than a statute or mandatory standard.

## Current examples (dated — refresh on review)

- **2026:** CISA / NSA and Five Eyes joint guidance, *Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services* (May 1, 2026) — coordinated government guidance on autonomous agents in critical infrastructure.
- **2026:** reporting on the March 1, 2026 drone strikes on AWS data centers in the UAE and Bahrain — the first confirmed military attacks on hyperscale cloud, and the event that carried data centers across the strategic-significance boundary.
- The **EU AI Act** high-risk regime, as the most developed regulatory treatment of AI in high-consequence settings as of this snapshot.
