# Pick Your Sector

> **Part of:** Core / Module 0

This is the one decision in the course that shapes every module after it. You are going to pick a critical-infrastructure sector now and carry it the whole way through — into every module's closing reflection, across the bridge where it stops being cosmetic, and into the capstone, where you apply the method to a real facility in it. Choose deliberately. You will be living with this choice.

## Why the choice threads through everything

The course teaches a method, and a method is only learned by using it on something concrete. Your sector is that something. Each core module ends with an **In Your Sector** block that asks you to take what you just learned and say something specific about your sector — so you are working your own material from the very first module, not waiting until the end to make it real. By the time you reach the capstone, you will have a running set of reflections about your sector that the final project draws on directly. Skip the sector choice, or keep it vague, and those reflections have nothing to bite on.

## Your sector does not need to be "ready"

The most important thing to understand before you choose: **your sector does not need to already have a formal design basis, a facility class, or any prebuilt governance instruments.** If it did, the founding gap wouldn't be a gap.

The nuclear, energy, and data-center material you will meet later are **worked examples** — the places where this discipline already exists or is actively being carved. They are there to show you the method in motion, not to fence off who the method is for. The method itself is sector-agnostic. If you work in water, communications, transportation, healthcare, financial services, chemical, or anywhere else, you walk the exact same core, you study the energy carve-outs as models, and your capstone becomes the more valuable exercise: *derive the facility class for a facility in my sector, from scratch.* That is not a consolation path. That is the method doing the thing it was built to do.

So do not pick based on where the frameworks already exist. Pick based on where you can reason well and where the stakes are real.

## Where this is heading

It helps to know now what the course will eventually ask you to produce, so your choice is made with the finish line in view. At the capstone you will take a real facility in your chosen sector and work *two* design bases for it: one for the facility itself, and a second for any AI operating inside it. You do not need to understand what that means yet — the two-design-bases idea is the keystone of Module 3, and the toolkit for the second one is Module 4. For now it is enough to know the target: not an essay about your sector, but the method applied to a real facility in it, twice. Pick a sector you would be glad to still be reasoning about when you get there.

## How to choose well

Three things make a sector productive to carry through this course.

**Choose one you know.** The method rewards concrete knowledge — of how the sector actually operates, what its critical functions are, and what would genuinely be catastrophic. Pick the sector where you can answer "what must never happen here?" without guessing. Your own working domain is almost always the right answer.

**Choose one where the consequences are real.** Design-basis thinking is a heavy instrument; it earns its keep where an outcome would be genuinely intolerable — a physical release, a loss of an essential service, a break in institutional trust. If a sector has a plausible must-not-happen outcome, it is a good fit. Nearly every critical-infrastructure sector does.

**Choose one where you can name a real facility.** At the capstone you will apply the method to a specific facility — a plant, a terminal, a station, a data center, a clearing system. You do not need to pick the exact facility now, but pick a sector where a real, nameable one exists in your view. Abstraction is where design-basis reasoning goes to die.

## Record your choice

Write your sector down somewhere you will keep. Add a sentence on why you picked it, and a first, rough answer to the question that will haunt the rest of the course: *what is the one outcome a facility in this sector must never produce?* You will refine that answer many times. Having a first version now gives every later module something to sharpen.

*(In a later live-form version of this course, this is the point where your sector is captured and stored so the course can track your progress. For now, you are your own store of record — keep your choice and your reflections together.)*

## In your sector

You have made the choice. Before moving on, state it in one line — *"I am carrying \_\_\_\_ through this course"* — and pair it with your first-draft answer to *what must never happen at a facility in this sector.* That single sentence and that single outcome are the seed the entire method grows from. Carry them into Module 1.
