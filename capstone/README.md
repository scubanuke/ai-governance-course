# Capstone — Apply the Method to a Facility in Your Sector

## Learning Objectives

After this module, a student will be able to:

- For a real facility in their sector, produce both design bases (OT) or a governance analysis reckoning with both (policy).
- Demonstrate the inherit-and-diverge relationship on a real target.
- Meet the shared rubric's gating requirement that both design bases be present.


Pick a real facility in the sector you chose at Module 0. Both branches analyze the **same** target facility; the deliverable differs by branch.

**Both design bases are required.** From Module 3 onward the course insists that a facility where AI operates has two design bases — the facility-class design basis (FC-DB) and the AI design basis (AI-DB). A capstone that produces only one is the incomplete answer the course exists to prevent. This holds for **both** branches:

- **OT learners derive both DBs** as engineering artifacts — the FC-DB and the AI-DB — and show the Bright Line seam between them.
- **Policy learners analyze both DBs** — the facility's consequence set and the AI's inherited-plus-extended set, its distinct adversary envelope, and the governance gap each implies.

Same requirement (address both design bases), different deliverable form.

**See the worked example first.** A fully worked pass through the whole method — for a water treatment plant, with short versions of both branch deliverables — is in [`resources/worked-example-water-treatment.md`](../resources/worked-example-water-treatment.md). Read it as a model answer before you start: it shows what "both design bases, joined at the Bright Line" looks like on a real facility, and the checklist at its end is exactly what the shared rubric grades.
